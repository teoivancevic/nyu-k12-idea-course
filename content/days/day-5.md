## 10:30–10:45 · Field Trip Preparation

Before we leave, spend a few minutes preparing. The founders at Newlab are building real physical products — hardware, manufacturing, deep tech. That's different from what you've been building this week, but the same questions apply: who has the problem, who pays, what does it cost to deliver.

Your job during the visit is to listen for the things that match or contradict what you've been learning. Bring at least one question from the list below to ask.

### Questions for the Founders

**Warm-up / origin**
- What problem were you personally frustrated by before you started this company?
- Why did you build a physical product instead of software or an app?

**Market and business model**
- Who is your first paying customer, and how did you find them?
- What's the hardest part about scaling something that requires factories or physical space, compared to scaling an app?
- Who pays for this — the end user, a government, or a business customer? Why?

**Decisions and tradeoffs**
- What's one version of this idea you tried and abandoned?
- What's a decision you made early on that you'd make differently now?

### Field Visit Reflection

Before the end of day, write down your answers to these three questions. There's no right answer — just be honest about what stuck with you.

1. Which founder's problem felt most like something you'd personally want to solve?
2. What's one thing you saw today that made you want to learn more — something you might look up once you're back home?
3. What's the equivalent "hard part" for your own pitch idea? What's one thing you saw today that changes how you think about your own project?
