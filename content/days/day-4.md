The professor just walked through all six blocks. Now build yours.

This is already done — it's carried directly from your problem statement. Copy it from your Day 2 doc into the worksheet. No prompt needed.

---

### Block 2 · Who Pays

```
Based on everything above, who would realistically pay to fund 
a solution to our problem — a government agency, a grant, a 
nonprofit, a subscription, or something else?

Is the payer the same person as the user, or different? 
Why does that matter for how we'd build this?

Two to three sentences. Be specific — name the type of funder, 
not just "the government."
```

---

### Block 3 · Value Proposition

```
Write our value proposition in one sentence. Not a list of 
features — one outcome the user would actually notice because 
this exists.

Format: "[Specific user] can now [do something they couldn't 
do before / avoid something that used to hurt them]."
```

---

### Block 4 · Key Partners & Channels

```
For a solution to our problem:
- Who do we need on our side to make this real? 
  (city agency, nonprofit, platform, regulator)
- How would people actually find out this exists and start 
  using it?

In public service, the partner is often the channel too — 
name that if it applies to us. Two to three sentences.
```

---

### Block 5 · Cost Structure

```
What would it actually cost to build and keep running a 
solution to our problem? Think about:
- Technology and hosting
- People needed to maintain it
- Data, compliance, or legal costs
- Translation or accessibility if relevant

Keep it to three to four bullet points. Be specific, not vague.
```

---

### Block 6 · Revenue & Funding Streams

```
Based on who pays (Block 2), describe exactly where the money 
comes from. Is it a grant, an annual government contract, 
a subscription, or something else?

Does this funding renew automatically, or does it have to be 
won again each year? What does that mean for how stable 
this solution would be?

Two to three sentences.
```

---

### Reconcile

```
Here are our six blocks: [paste your completed BMC].
Here is our SOM from Day 3: [paste your SOM number and source].

Does our funding source actually support that SOM number? 
If our funding is one grant or contract, is our SOM capped 
by that budget rather than by total demand?

Name the gap honestly if there is one. One paragraph.
```

## US College Majors: A Starting Point

A few things worth understanding before diving in:

---

### How US Undergraduate Education Works

Before looking at specific programs, five things that work differently than you might expect:

**You don't declare your major before you arrive.** At most US universities you spend your first two years taking courses across different subjects, then formally declare your major at the end of sophomore year. You are not locked in by what you write on your application.

**A major is only part of your degree.** Roughly 10–15 courses out of 40 total required to graduate. The rest are electives and general education requirements. You can major in Computer Science and still take economics, design, or public policy. Double majors and minors are common and don't require extra years if planned carefully.

**You can change your major.** In China, switching majors after admission is difficult and rare. In the US it is normal — many students change once or twice. If you're unsure what to study, that is not a reason to delay applying.

**Rankings measure universities, not programs.** The US News overall ranking tells you about graduation rates, faculty resources, and research output — it does not tell you how strong a specific program is. Michigan State ranks around #80 overall but has the #1 supply chain program in the country. Always research the specific program within the university, not just the institution's name.

**The application timeline is earlier than you think:**

| When | What |
|---|---|
| 11th grade (now, if applicable) | SAT/ACT prep, start college list, think about essay topics |
| Summer before 12th grade | Draft your Common App personal statement (650 words, takes 2–3 months to get right) |
| September–October of 12th grade | Request recommendation letters (give teachers 6–8 weeks minimum) |
| November 1–15 | Early Decision (binding) and Early Action (non-binding) deadlines |
| January 1–15 | Regular Decision deadlines for most schools |
| March–April | Decisions arrive, compare offers carefully |
| May 1 | National Decision Day — deadline to commit |

**One thing international students often miss:** financial aid exists for international applicants but varies enormously by school. A small number of schools — MIT, Harvard, Yale, Princeton — are need-blind for international students and meet 100% of demonstrated financial need. Most schools are not this generous. Research each school's international student aid policy specifically before applying.

#### Engineering

**What you study:** depends heavily on subfield. Mechanical, electrical, civil, biomedical, chemical, aerospace, industrial, environmental — each is a different degree with different coursework. Most schools require you to specify a subfield when you apply.

**Subfield matters more than most students realize.** Georgia Tech has the #1 industrial and systems engineering program in the country (25 consecutive years), the #1 environmental engineering program, and the #1 biomedical engineering program — but is #3 overall. If you know your subfield, research that ranking specifically.

**Top programs (US News 2026 undergraduate engineering ranking):**

| School | US News Rank | Notable For | Website |
|---|---|---|---|
| MIT | [#1](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/engineering) | Strongest across all subfields | [mit.edu](https://www.mit.edu) |
| Stanford | [#2](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/engineering) | Interdisciplinary, Silicon Valley | [stanford.edu](https://www.stanford.edu) |
| UC Berkeley | [#3](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/engineering) | Top public, strong research | [berkeley.edu](https://www.berkeley.edu) |

| Purdue | [#5](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/engineering) | Aerospace, mechanical, nuclear | [purdue.edu](https://www.purdue.edu) |
| Carnegie Mellon | [#7](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/engineering) | Robotics, CS-engineering crossover | [cmu.edu](https://www.cmu.edu) |

**Hidden gems:**
- [**WPI (Worcester Polytechnic Institute)**](https://www.wpi.edu) — top-35 nationally for ROI and career outcomes. Every student completes a real-world project with a company or government agency as a graduation requirement — not a thesis, an actual deliverable. Boston-area location. [CollegeVine ranks it top-10 underrated for engineering](https://blog.collegevine.com/top-10-most-underrated-engineering-colleges-in-the-us/).
- [**Stevens Institute of Technology**](https://www.stevens.edu) — Hoboken, NJ (directly across from Manhattan). STEM training that rivals the Ivy League according to multiple rankings. Strong finance-technology crossover programs given the NYC proximity. Often overlooked internationally.
- [**University of Houston**](https://www.uh.edu) — top-50 nationally for engineering ROI, top-200 overall. Strong in chemical, petroleum, and mechanical engineering. Guaranteed admission for students in the top 10% of their high school class.

#### Business, Finance & Economics

**What you study:** accounting, finance, marketing, management, operations, entrepreneurship. Business is one of the most common majors for international students at US universities.

**Important admissions note:** some top business programs are direct-admit from high school (Wharton, NYU Stern, Notre Dame). Others require a second internal application during your sophomore year (Berkeley Haas, UVA McIntire, UNC Kenan-Flagler). If the business school is your goal, research the specific admissions pathway — getting into the university doesn't guarantee getting into the business school.

**Top programs by specialty (US News 2026):**

| Specialty | #1 Program | Other Top Programs |
|---|---|---|
| Finance | [Wharton (Penn)](https://www.wharton.upenn.edu) | [NYU Stern](https://www.stern.nyu.edu), [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu), [Michigan Ross](https://michiganross.umich.edu) |
| Supply Chain | [Michigan State Broad](https://broad.msu.edu) | [Penn State Smeal](https://smeal.psu.edu), [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu) |
| Accounting | [UT Austin McCombs](https://www.mccombs.utexas.edu) | [Illinois Gies](https://giesbusiness.illinois.edu), [BYU Marriott](https://marriottschool.byu.edu) |
| Entrepreneurship | [Babson](https://www.babson.edu) | MIT Sloan, Stanford |
| International Business | [South Carolina Moore](https://sc.edu/study/colleges_schools/moore/) | [NYU Stern](https://www.stern.nyu.edu), [Wharton](https://www.wharton.upenn.edu) |

**Hidden gems:**
- [**Indiana University Kelley School of Business**](https://kelley.iu.edu) — consistently [top-10 for undergraduate business](https://www.usnews.com/best-colleges/rankings/business-overall), often ranked above schools with much more famous institutional names. Strong placement in investment banking, consulting, and accounting. Finance specialty ranked top-5 nationally. The overall university is mid-tier but the business school significantly outperforms it.
- [**Michigan State Broad**](https://broad.msu.edu) — #1 supply chain program in the country, year after year. If supply chain, operations, or logistics is your interest, this outranks every Ivy League school in that specialty.
- [**George Mason University**](https://www2.gmu.edu) — one of the most influential economics departments in the US (home of two Nobel laureates in economics). Comparable career outcomes to GWU and American University at significantly lower cost and with less competitive admissions.

#### Data Science & Statistics

**What you study:** probability, statistical modeling, machine learning, data analysis, visualization. Closely related to CS but more focused on working with data than building systems.

**Why it's increasingly important:** almost every field — medicine, finance, government, social science — now needs people who can analyze data. A statistics or data science degree is unusually flexible in terms of career options.

**Top programs:**

| School | Notable For | Website |
|---|---|---|

| [MIT](https://www.mit.edu) | Quantitative rigor, research | [mit.edu](https://www.mit.edu) |
| [Stanford](https://www.stanford.edu) | Statistics + industry connections | [stanford.edu](https://www.stanford.edu) |
| [Columbia](https://www.columbia.edu) | NYC location, finance and tech applications | [columbia.edu](https://www.columbia.edu) |
| [University of Michigan](https://umich.edu) | Strong public option, broad data science program | [umich.edu](https://umich.edu) |

**Hidden gem:**
- [**University of Rochester**](https://www.rochester.edu) — small research university, strong quantitative and statistics programs, historically more generous with financial aid for international students than many comparable schools. Often overlooked because it's not in a major city.

#### Design & Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)

**What you study:** varies significantly. Pure design programs focus on visual communication, product design, and UX. HCI programs combine design with psychology and computer science — how humans interact with technology.

**Important to know:** the best design programs in the US are often at art schools, not universities. RISD and Parsons are more respected for pure design than most Ivy League schools. If you want design + technology combined, look for HCI programs specifically.

**Notable programs:**

| School | Notable For | Website |
|---|---|---|
| [RISD (Rhode Island School of Design)](https://www.risd.edu) | Most respected pure design school in the US | [risd.edu](https://www.risd.edu) |
| [Parsons School of Design (The New School)](https://www.newschool.edu/parsons/) | NYC location, fashion and product design | [newschool.edu/parsons](https://www.newschool.edu/parsons/) |
| [Carnegie Mellon HCI](https://www.hcii.cmu.edu) | Gold standard for design + technology combined | [hcii.cmu.edu](https://www.hcii.cmu.edu) |
| [Georgia Tech](https://www.cc.gatech.edu) | Strong CS + design crossover | [gatech.edu](https://www.gatech.edu) |

**Hidden gem:**
- [**Northeastern University**](https://www.northeastern.edu) — one of the fastest-rising schools in the US by employer reputation and admission competitiveness. Strong combined CS + design programs, and the mandatory co-op program (paid work experience built into the degree) is among the best in the country for connecting students to real jobs before graduation. [Rising star for 2025–2026 according to multiple ranking sources](https://collegerover.com/campus-library/news/377/rising-stars-among-underrated-colleges).
